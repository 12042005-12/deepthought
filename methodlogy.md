# Methodology

## City Selected

Hyderabad


## Segments Selected

1. Specialty Diagnostics & Life Science Tools
2. Industrial Sensors & Instrumentation
3. Precision Engineering


## Sources Used

- Company Websites
- LinkedIn
- Google News
- Tofler
- Naukri
- Annual Reports


## Process Followed

Initially, I identified around 35 companies operating in Hyderabad within the selected segments.

I applied the E1 criteria to determine whether the company was a producer or manufacturer.

I then applied the E2 criteria to assess whether the company was accessible through public information.

The remaining companies were evaluated using the Federer framework.

The criteria considered included differentiation, decision-maker quality, growth potential, growth signals, systems maturity, and leadership continuity.

Based on these scores, the top 25 companies were selected.


## Challenges

Revenue information was not publicly available for some companies.

Succession planning information required further verification.

Some growth signals were inferred from hiring activity and expansion announcements.


## Learnings

Companies with structured manufacturing systems, ERP adoption, and global certifications generally scored higher.

The exercise helped me understand how qualitative signals can be used to identify promising businesses.
